## 1. Context in Clinical Machine Learning.mp4

### Summary

This text examines the tension between **high-performance deep learning** and the need for **model interpretability** within clinical settings. Because complex algorithms often act as **"black boxes"** that lack human-like common sense or context, they risk relying on **spurious correlations** that could lead to patient harm. To address this, the source outlines a trade-off where simpler, **intrinsically interpretable models** like decision trees are easier to trust, while powerful neural networks require **post-hoc explanation methods**—such as saliency maps—to visualize which data points drive a prediction. Ultimately, the discussion questions whether AI should be held to a **higher standard of transparency** than human clinicians or established medical treatments, emphasizing that **multidisciplinary teams** remain essential for safely deploying these sophisticated tools.

**Keywords:** Clinical machine learning, Model interpretability, Black box models, Saliency maps, Multidisciplinary teams
